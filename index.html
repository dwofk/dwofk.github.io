About Me

I am a Masters of Engineering student in the EECS department at MIT, working in the Energy-Efficient Multimedia Systems Group. I am advised by Prof. Vivienne Sze. My research interests lie in algorithm-hardware co-design for low-power low-latency computer vision and machine learning. My current focus is on learning-based depth estimation with applications to robot navigation. I received my Bachelor of Science in EECS from MIT in 2018.

Resume available upon request.
You can reach me at dwofk at mit dot edu.

Research

I first joined the MIT Energy-Efficient Multimedia Systems Group as a freshman participating in the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program. My earlier projects focused on communication interfaces between PCs, FPGAs, and embedded computing platforms. As part of the 2017-2018 SuperUROP cohort, I began working on low-latency learning-based depth estimation research. My work was recognized as one of the top three projects completed that year. I am continuing to expand on this work as part of my masters research.
